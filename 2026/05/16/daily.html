🥇 For $1.3 million a month, OpenClaw founder runs 100 AI agents that code, review PRs, and find bugs

OpenClaw’s Peter Steinberger spends $1.3M monthly on 100 AI agents handling coding, PR reviews, and bug detection — The setup automates large parts of software development but raises questions about cost sustainability.

Why it matters: This is the first public benchmark of what it actually costs to run a fully agentic dev team at scale.

Researchers train AI model that hits near-full performance with just 12.5 percent of its experts

A new modular AI model achieves 95% of full performance while activating only 12.5% of its expert modules — The approach could drastically reduce inference costs without sacrificing accuracy.

Why it matters: If scalable, this could make large models far cheaper to run in production.

Google says GEO and AEO are a myth and traditional SEO is all you need for AI search

Google claims traditional SEO practices remain sufficient for AI-powered search, dismissing “Generative Engine Optimization” (GEO) and “Answer Engine Optimization” (AEO) as unnecessary — The company argues its AI search still relies on established ranking signals.

Why it matters: If true, it simplifies the playbook for marketers—but skepticism remains about Google’s motives.
