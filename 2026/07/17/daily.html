🥇 Mistral AI unveils Mistral-Next-123B, surpassing Llama 3.1 on key benchmarks

Mistral AI open-sourced its largest model to date, clocking in at 123 billion parameters and outperforming Meta’s Llama 3.1 405B on MMLU and coding tasks. The model is available under a permissive Apache 2.0 license, with weights hosted on Hugging Face.

Why it matters: The gap between open and closed models just narrowed—enterprise adoption of open-weight systems may accelerate if performance parity holds.

EU regulators fine DeepMind €120M for AI Act violation

The European Commission issued its first penalty under the AI Act, slapping DeepMind with a €120 million fine for failing to disclose training data sources in its Gemini Ultra model. The company has 30 days to comply or face additional daily penalties.

Why it matters: The ruling sets a precedent for transparency requirements—other labs may preemptively audit their datasets to avoid similar scrutiny.

Anthropic slashes inference costs with in-house TPU alternative

Anthropic announced “Claude Chips,” custom silicon optimized for its models, reducing cloud spend by 40% compared to Nvidia A100s. The chips, co-designed with Broadcom, will be available to select cloud partners later this year.

Why it matters: Vertical integration is becoming table stakes—expect more labs to follow suit as hardware costs eat into margins.

Chinese labs release multimodal agent benchmark

A consortium of Chinese AI labs published “AgentBench-MM”, a new benchmark for evaluating multimodal agents across 50 real-world tasks, from web navigation to robotics. Early results show domestic models like SenseTime’s SenseNova leading in task completion rates.

Why it matters: Agentic AI is the next frontier—this benchmark could become the de facto standard for measuring progress.
